Input-Output Channels

Vision, hearing and touch are central.

Vision

We can divide visual perception into two stages:

  1. the physical perception of the stimulus from the outside world.
  2. the procssing and interpretation of that stimulus

Components of human eyes
1]Cornea, Lens
2]Retina is light sensitive and contains two types of photoreceptor:

  • rods: vision at low light level, do not mediate color vision, low spatial acuity
  • cones: vision higher light level, capable of color vision, high spatial acuity

Fovea takes less than 1% of retinal size but takes up over 50% of the visual cortex in the brain.

Red-green color blindness happens more in males than in females.

Perception is not seeing as how we interpret depends on how we understand what our eyes tell us.

Interpretation

  • Context is important
  • Visual system compensates for movement and changes in luminance
  • Optical illusion == over compensation (视错觉==过度补偿)

Hearing

Ear includes:

  1. outer ear
  2. middle ear
  3. inner ear

Key sounds variations

  • pitch(强度) : sound frequency
  • loudness : amplitude(大小)
  • timbre(音质): type or quality

It provides information about the environment such as distances, directions, objects etc.

Frequency that human can hear is between 20Hz to 15kHz, and it becomes less accurate when distinguishing higher Hz sounds.

Filtering

  1. can hear sounds over background noise
  2. cocktail party effect: concentration on some perticular sound among sounds.
  3. selective attention

Touch

Touch is about receiving feedback from environment, it is particularly important when visual sense impairs.

Stimulus from skin receptors:

  1. thermoreceptors: hot and cold
  2. nociceptors: pain
    3.mechanoreceptors: pressure

Kinesthetic and Proprioceptive Senses(动觉和本体感觉)

Proprioception is about one’ own body
Kinesthetic sense is about receptors in muscle firbres and joints. (Awareness of the position of the body and limbs. Haptic reception)

Vestibular sense: balance and movement from semicircular canals in the ear
Visual sense: relation of body parts to each other and object in the world.

Movement

Human Memory

Memory is constructed by three parts:

  • Sensory memory
  • Short-term memory/ working memory
  • Long-termn memory

Sensory Memory

Momentary store for stimuli received by the sense
Modality specific(i.e limited to 1 sense)
Quickly lost if not encoded in STM

Short-term memory/ Working memory

STM degrades quickly and has limited capacity

  • Quick access time (70ms)
  • Short term storage(200ms,10-20 second decay time)
  • limited capacity
  • Store in LTM by rehearsal

STM holds information that has been used,
A chunck is a single object conveys a larger amount of information, e.g. 123 456 789, shapes and colors…

We typically can hold 7+-2 chunks in short term memory. And chunking information can increase the short-term memory capacity.

Closure: successful formation of a chunk

  • Desire to complete stuff
  • clear memory stack

example: ATM, get your card first, then take your money.

Long-term memory

LTM has totally four types in two catagories:
1] Explicit(declarative)

  • Episodic(events)
  • Semantic(knowledge, concepts)

2] Implicit(non-declarative)

  • Procedural(skills, actions)
  • Emotional conditioning

LTM has the following features:

  • Huge capacity
  • hold information for a long time
  • slow access time
  • better at recognition than recall

Activities of LTM:

  1. Storage or remembering (can be improved by familarity, repetition and distribution of practice effect)
  2. forgetting (by decay or interference)
  3. information retrieval (assist recall by providing retrieval cues)

Mental Models

def: an internal model of the relationships and structures between external real world things

  • The model is used for reasoning, problem solving…
  • They are based on belief.
  • Model helps simplify decision making

Learning

Mental models are often incorrect, we can update our model when it is wrong.
