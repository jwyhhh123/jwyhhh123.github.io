Understanding Users and Interviews

WY J
2020-10-20 (Updated: 2020-10-24)
Year3/ Human Computer Interaction

Involving users in the design process

Why is it important to involve users in the design process?

  • gain better understanting of user goals to produce usable product
  • user expectation management
  • give user ownership

User Involvement in the Design Cycle

1] Informers

  • gather information from users
  • This information guides design
  • user involves in testing of prototypes
  • effortful or low effort to user

2] Cusumer

  • they are given newsletters
  • invite users to workshops/events
  • users are not so involved

3] Designer

  • participatory design
  • however users are not experts
  • part time or full time involvement
  • time cost

Requirements

def: statements specify what a product should do or how it should perform.

Requirements should be specific, unambiguous and clear

examples:

  1. user characteristics: users(children, adults, disabled…) should be able to see and reach the tabletop display.
  2. usability: visitors should find the tabletop engaging.

Software Engineering

Functional: what the system should do
Non-functional: how it should do it/constraints on the development of the
product

Interaction Design

Functional: to capture functionality required and constraints under which it
should operate or be developed

Data and environmental requirements:

  • how the system handles users data
  • circumstances/ contexts of the product (physical, technical, social…)

What do users need?

Users may not know what they need, so we have to understand:

  • User characteristics
  • User capabilities/ physical characteristics
  • User task and current methods
  • Whether new design would lead to improvements

Task-Artifect Cycle

Gathering requirements

def: Gathering data to better understand users’ needs, current methods, context. And use these findings to generate requirements for design.

1) Establish goals of study
2) Identify participants(sampling)

  • representatives
  • motivations for participation

3) Approaches to gather requirements:

  1. Interview
  2. Focus groups
  3. Questionnaires and surveys
  4. Obsevation
  5. Experience sampling
  6. Diaries
  7. Cultural probes
  8. Study documentation
  9. Research similar products

Important issues to consider

Sampling
Piloting
Triangulation
Trust, privacy and ethics

Interviews

Planning phase:

  • Plan questions
  • Choose participants
  • Agree locations
  • Agree time and length
  • Recording and analysis
  • Gather requirements

Interview Structure:

  1. Structured
  2. Unstructured
  3. Semi-Structured
  4. Group(focus groups)

where focus groups are interviewing in groups, usually used in marketing, social researching, campaigning. It can involve a selection of stakeholders(利益相关者).

Stages of interview:

  1. Background
  2. Letting off steam
  3. Addressing issues
  4. Typing up/debriefing

Questionnaires

Four main types of questions:

  1. simple factual questions (y/n)
  2. complex factual questions
  3. opinion and attitudinal questions
  4. open-ended questions

Likert scale:
gives a measure of how strongly someone agrees or disagrees with a question.
provides numerical values for data that isn’t actually measurable or recordable in another way
It is an easy way to record results.

NOTE: Likert scale without neutral answer forces users to make a choice. However, it may affect results when neutral answer is a highly relevant answer.

Advantages:

  • collect data from large number of people,
  • data is structured and easy to compare.

Disadvantages:

  • cannot follow up intriguing answers
  • answers are meaningless if questions are bad
  • hard to get people to engage with it
  • sampling problem

Comman mistakes in questionnaires

  1. Leading questions
  2. Assumptive/loaded
  3. Multi-part/double-barrelled
  4. Double negatives
  5. Jargon
  6. Mismatched scales
  7. Multiple/missing answers
  8. Random
  9. Irrelevant
  10. Inconsistent
  11. Unclear/multiple interpretations
  12. Won’t get honest answer (Do you mind me ask these questions?)
