def: Human computer interaction is a discipline concerned with the design, evaluation and implementation of interactive computing systems for human use and with the study of major phenomena surrounding them.

What is Interaction Design about?

Trade-offs
Design is about trade-offs, about balancing conflicting requirements.

Four basic activities:
1] Identifying needs and establishing requirements
2] Developing alternative designs
3] Building interactive versions of the designs
4] Evaluating designs

Three characteristics of its process
1] Focus on users
2] Specific usability and user experience goals

3] Iteration

Users: Identify who interact directively with the product to achieve the task. There are also potential users, so they can be classified into:

  1. Primary users
  2. secondary users
  3. tertiary users

Needs: It requires our understainding of users’ characteristics and capabilities(physically or culturally), what they are trying to achieve, how they achieve it currently, whether their goals can be improved.

Alternative Designs:
1] Creativity
2] Alternatives come from looking at other, similar designs.

How to choose among alternative designs?

Choosing among alternatives is about making decisions, and the decisions are classified into:

  • externally visible and measurable features (size, amount, height…)
  • characteristics internal to the system that is not visible (e.g. capacity of power supply, response time…)

However in interactive design, we concentrate on externally visible/ measurable behaviour. Internal behavior is also considered when it affects the external behavior.

Prototying is useful in the designing process as it is provided as a tool to test and answer questions about technical feasibility.

Lifecycle models

Software Engineering:

Waterfall lifecycle model

def: a linear model in which each step must be completed before the next step.

It focuses on the functionalities of the product.

Disadvantages:

  • specification set too early
  • assumes people know what they want
  • iteration was not embedded, the oppotunity to review and evaluate with users was not built into it.

Spiral lifecycle model

Spiral model has two features: risk analysis and prototyping. It incorporates them in an iterative framework that allows ideas and progress to be repeatly checked and evaluated.

It focuses on risks in development rather than the intended functionality.
It encourages alternative designs.

Rapid Application Development

Rapid Application development attempts to take a user-centered view and to minimize the risk caused by requirements changing. It has two key features:

  1. time-limited cycles of approximately six months. It breaks down a large project into smaller projects by time boxing.
  2. Joint Application Development where users and developers com together to thrash out the requirements.

Disadvantages:

  1. user involvement appears to be limited in JAD workshop
  2. iteration appears to be limited to design and build phase.

Human Computer Interaction:

###The Star lifecycle model###
Unlike the lifecycle models above, it does not specify any ordering of activities, it is highly interconnected.

Evaluation is central to this model, and whenever an activity is completed, its results must be evaluated.

The Usability Engineering Lifecycle

The lifecycle of this mdoel has three tasks:

  1. Requirements analysis
  2. Design/testing/development
  3. Installation
