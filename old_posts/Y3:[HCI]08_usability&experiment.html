Evaluation

Why

  • Determine how usable interaction is
  • Identify good and bad features to inform designs
  • Compare design choices
  • Observe effects of specific interfaces on users (they may do something you never expected)
  • to ensure the system is well designed

You are not the user because you know how to use it.

When to evaluate
Early on, Formative:

  • check that design meet user needs
  • when we are sketching and prototyping

Later on, Summative:

  • test things to see what needs to be improved
  • evaluate whether reached criteria

Types of Evaluation:

  1. Controlled settings with users
  2. Not involving users, heuristic,expert evaluation,walkthroughs,user modelling…
  3. Natural settings with users, field study,ethnography…

Types of evaluation you need depends on when in design cycle and what you want to find out.

ALL of these methods are for the below: Test what

  • Interview

think aloud

• A technique for understanding what users are thinking as they are conducting a task
• E.g. using a web interface
• Trying out your WoOz prototype system
• Think- Aloud protocol, adapted from Erikson and Simon(1985)
• Ask people to say out loud everything they are thinking and trying to do, so their thought processes are externalised

Advantages:

  • It allows us to find out what the user is thinking
  • why are they doing that
  • what are they looking for
  • what odes their overall mental model look like

How:

  1. prompt user to think aloud when they go quite because they start struggling
  2. take notes
  3. let the user make errors, let them get stuck

Instructions to participants

  • What are you looking for?
  • What do you think you need to do?
  • How do you think you’ll do it?
  • Can you see what you expect?
  • What can you not find?

what we want:
• “I want to do…”
• “I’m looking at the UI, and I think it does…”
• ‘Hmm, that’s not what I expected; I thought it was going to…”
• “That took longer than I expected.”

Don’t want:
• “I think the background should be blue.”
• “I don’t think other users are going to understand this…”
• “I would put this field over there.”

Disavantages: Not always possible as:

  • It is cognitively demanding tasks
  • speech tasks where people are talking

Alternatively, we can

  1. Think after with video playback
  2. Observation with occasional questions throughout; Questions after

Expert Analysis

It is relatively cheap and efficient

Inspection

  1. Cognitive walkthrough
  2. Heuristic evaluation

Other methods

NOTE: these are conducted by experts not users, for example UX experts

Cognitive walkthrough

  1. Expert evaluators ‘step through’ a sequence of actions/tasks with a prototype/specification of a system to evaluate its usability.
  2. Produce a report outlining what needs to be improved

Heuristic Evaluation

Def: a guideline or a rule of thumb

These heuristics (usability principles) can be used to guide design, or critique a design that has already been
made

Neilson’s 10 principles of heuristic evaluation

  1. Visibility of system status
  2. Match between system and real world
  3. User control and freedom
  4. Consistency and standards
  5. Error prevention
  6. Recognition rather than recall
  7. Flexibility and efficiency of use
  8. Aesthetic and minimalist design
  9. Help users recognise, diagnose and recover from errors
  10. Help and documentation

3 stages:

  1. Briefing session to tell experts what to do
  2. evaluation period of 1-2 hours in which each expert works separately, take one pass…take second pass…
  3. debriefing session in which experts work together to prioritise problems

Severity ratings is a combination of 3 factors: 0-4

  1. the frequency with which the problem occurs: is it common or rare?
  2. The impace of the problem
  3. The persistence of the problem: one-time or repeatedly be bothered?

Pros: cheap and easy, quick, fits in with software development process
Cons:

  • difficult to find experts
  • important problems may get missed
  • biases in experts

Model based evaluation

Using previous studies in evaluation

Refer to the literature: making use of existing knowledge, experimental results and empiricl evidence

but results don’t hold in all contexts. need an expert to find sim and diff between experimental context and the current design.

Discussion point

How many users do you need to evaluate your system?

Method: to run your experiment with as many people you can find as possible. Check what additional information you can get. Stop when no more info.
